
Some good resources for learning R:


-- A great place to start is at the R project site:  http://r-project.org

   There is an excellent "Introduction to R" among the many R manuals at:
      http://cran.r-project.org/manuals.html
   
      There is even a translation of the Introduction to R in Chinese:
      http://www.biosino.org/R/R-doc/
   
   The site also has a list of short R tutorial writeups
      (many in languages other than English) at:
      http://cran.r-project.org/other-docs.html
   
   This site also lists other documentation, including books about R:
      http://www.r-project.org/other-docs.html
   

-- There are good tutorial videos about R online.

   A nice sequence of tutorial videos by developers.google.com is at:
      https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOU2XLYxmsIK9qQfztXeybpHvru-TrqAP


-- If you want more advanced how-to books, with code snippets and
   semi-real demos, O'Reilly has a large set of books about R:

   http://search.oreilly.com/?q=R

   UCLA used to have free access to Safari, O'Reilly's online access portal:
   http://learnit.ucla.edu/safari
